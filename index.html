About Me

My Skills

Python HTML CSS JavaScript Scheme (Racket) SQL Git & GitHub Docker (Swift Basics)

I'm Mikolaj Mikuliszyn, a first-year Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence student at the University of Greenwich. My work focuses on exploring the intersection between system design, cognitive modeling, and the future of machine intelligence. I'm particularly interested in how we simulate thought, emotion, and memory in artificial systems.

I've developed a wide range of projects — from prime-finding algorithms and family tree models, to AI-powered Discord bots and experimental architectures exploring emotional behavior in machine learning. Each project serves as a step toward building systems that respond intelligently to their environment and evolve through interaction.

Beyond AI, I've worked on simulations involving physics and orbital mechanics, game development in Xcode, backend systems using Python and SQL, and early-stage ideas in quantum-assisted cognition. My approach is grounded in curiosity and driven by the belief that understanding the limits of artificial intelligence can teach us something deeper about our own.

Right now, I'm working to close the gap between where I am and where I want to be — aiming to reach the level of the best student engineers in the world. That means building consistently, writing and refining whitepapers, and pushing my understanding past just the technical layer. I care less about perfect execution and more about constant, forward momentum — every line of code, every strange idea, every late-night thought experiment contributes to something bigger.
